Six elements. Each one doing a job.

Credibility
"Licensed electrician"
Licensing is a trust signal — it tells the client this pro is verified, insured, and accountable. It answers "can I trust this person in my home?" before the client even asks.
Experience
"12 years of experience"
A specific number beats "experienced" or "been doing this for a while." It's verifiable and concrete — the client can picture the depth of work behind it.
Specificity
"Residential rewiring and panel upgrades"
This tells a homeowner with a rewiring need: "this is my person." Generic phrases like "electrical services" don't create that match. Specificity converts because clients self-select into the service they need.
Location
"Denver metro area"
Clients want someone who works in their area and knows local codes. Mentioning the service area in the intro — not just in a sidebar — makes it feel personal and immediate.
Social proof
"Over 200 five-star reviews"
Review counts are the strongest conversion signal on the platform. Pulling the number into the intro means the client sees it even if they never scroll down to the reviews section.
Differentiator
"Same-week availability"
Most clients hiring an electrician have an urgent need. "Same-week availability" is the reason to reach out now instead of bookmarking and comparing. It's the element that turns browsing into action.

What you're looking for when you edit AI output

When the AI Bio Booster generates a draft, these are the six categories you're checking for. A strong intro has most of them; a weak intro is usually missing three or more. Your job as the specialist isn't to add elements the pro can't back up — it's to make sure the real ones are in the intro where clients will actually see them.
